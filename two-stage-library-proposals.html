Traditionally, calls for proposals for new C++ standard library components have required "Proposed Text for the Standard" in the form of full standardese. This requirement discourages the submission of worthwhile proposals:

Writing standardese is daunting for those who do not have experience with this very specialized language. Learning to write standardese just isn't worth it for a proposal that may be rejected anyhow.

Writing standardese is very time consuming, even for those who have written a lot of standardese. Requiring full standardese for a proposal that may be rejected anyhow is unreasonable, particularly for large and complex libraries.

Yet for smaller proposals it may be possible to find volunteers to write the actual standardese, and for larger proposals volunteers can help a library author learn the basics and review wording. But the current procedure creates a chicken-and-egg problem; a proposal is needed to attract volunteers, but without volunteers it is too hard to write a proposal.
